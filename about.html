About the New York Knicks

Discover the legacy, champions, and future stars of the Knicks.

Rich History

Established in 1946 as one of the NBA’s charter franchises, the Knicks have played at Madison Square Garden for over 70 years—cementing their place in New York City culture.

Championships & Legends
  • • NBA Champions: 1970, 1973
  • • Walt “Clyde” Frazier: Iconic floor general
  • • Patrick Ewing: Franchise cornerstone
  • • Willis Reed: 1970 Finals MVP
Modern Era

Recent seasons have brought new stars like Jalen Brunson and Mikal Bridges, reigniting fan passion and hope for another championship run.

Franchise Timeline

1946

Knicks founded as a charter member of the NBA.

1970

First NBA Championship, led by Willis Reed and Walt Frazier.

1994–1999

Patrick Ewing era: multiple playoff runs and deep postseason runs.

2021–Present

Rebuild pays off: Playoff return in 2021 and resurgence under new core.
