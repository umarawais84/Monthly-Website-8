About the New York Knicks

The New York Knicks are one of the oldest teams in the National Basketball Association, having been founded in 1946. Since their inception, they’ve played in Madison Square Garden and have become an iconic franchise in the heart of New York City.

Over the decades, the Knicks have captured two NBA championships (1970, 1973) and have produced legendary talents like Walt “Clyde” Frazier, Patrick Ewing, and Willis Reed. The team’s signature blue and orange colors represent the vibrant spirit of New York.

In recent years, the roster has seen a revival with exciting young talent and veteran leadership. Fans continue to flock to Madison Square Garden, making it one of the most electric arenas in all of sports.
